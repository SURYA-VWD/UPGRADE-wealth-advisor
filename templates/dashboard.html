SIP Calculator – Systematic Investment Plan Calculator

Prospective investors can think that SIPs and mutual funds are the same. However, SIPs are merely a method of investing in mutual funds, the other method being a lump sum.

What is a SIP Calculator?

A SIP calculator is a simple tool that allows individuals to get an idea of the returns on their mutual fund investments made through SIP. SIP investments in mutual funds have become one of the most popular investment options for investors lately. These calculators give potential investors an estimate on their mutual fund wealth growth over long horizons.

How Does the Calculation Work? (Formula Explained)

The calculator uses compound interest formula calculated monthly to project portfolio growth:

M = P × [{(1 + i)^n - 1} / i] × (1 + i)

Where M is the final amount you receive upon maturity, P is the amount you invest every month, i is the periodic rate of interest (annual rate / 12 / 100), and n is the total number of monthly payments.

Key Benefits & Advantages

  • Rupee Cost Averaging: You buy more fund units when market prices are low and fewer units when prices are high, lowering your average cost.
  • Power of Compounding: Returns earned on your investments earn returns themselves, compounding wealth exponentially over long durations.
  • Disciplined Financial Habit: Automated monthly deductions ensure regular savings without relying on market timing.
  • Flexibility & Low Entry Barrier: Start investing with as little as ₹500/month and pause or step up contributions as your income grows.
